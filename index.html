Proven Longevity Compounds

Strong RCT & Meta-Analysis Evidence

Vitamin D3 (Cholecalciferol)

800 – 2,000 IU / day
Genomic Stability • Immune Regulation • Anti-Inflammaging

Acts as a secosteroid hormone influencing ~3% of the human genome via the Vitamin D Receptor (VDR). Reduces oxidative stress and chronic inflammaging, preserving telomere length and genomic stability. Essential for bone homeostasis, innate immune function, and endocrine regulation.

Key Evidence
  • Meta-analysis of RCTs (N=74,655): statistically significant reduction in cancer mortality (RR 0.85); all-cause mortality RR 0.98.
  • VITAL trial (N=25,871): emerging signal for reduced cancer mortality (HR 0.83).
  • Systematic review (Zhang et al., 2019, BMJ): Vitamin D3 supplementation associated with decreased total mortality across 52 RCTs.
  • Meta-analysis of 10 RCTs: significantly reduced total cancer mortality (RR 0.87, 95% CI 0.79–0.96, p = 0.005) (Keum et al., 2019).
Zhang 2019 arrow_outward Keum 2019 arrow_outward DOI: 10.1136/bmj.l4673

Omega-3 Fatty Acids (EPA + DHA)

1 – 4 g EPA+DHA / day
Pro-Resolving Mediators • NF-kB Inhibition • Telomere Preservation

EPA and DHA serve as precursors for specialized pro-resolving mediators (SPMs) like resolvins and protectins, which actively terminate inflammatory responses. They modulate cell signaling by inhibiting the pro-inflammatory NF-kB pathway and preserve telomere length by mitigating oxidative DNA damage.

Key Evidence
  • Pooled analysis of 17 prospective studies (N=42,466, Nature Communications): 18% reduction in all-cause mortality associated with higher blood omega-3 levels over 16-year median follow-up (Harris et al., 2021).
  • DO-HEALTH study (N=777): Omega-3s reduced biological age across multiple epigenetic clocks (PhenoAge, GrimAge2) and slowed the pace of aging.
  • Meta-analysis of 38 RCTs: confirmed significant reduction in cardiovascular mortality.
  • Network meta-analysis of 55 RCTs: significant CVD death reduction (RR 0.92, 95% CI 0.87–0.98) (Kim et al., 2020).
Harris 2021 arrow_outward Kim 2020 arrow_outward DOI: 10.1038/s41467-021-22370-2

Magnesium

250 – 450 mg / day
300+ Enzymatic Reactions • DNA Repair • Calcium Channel Blockade

Essential catalytic cofactor for over 300 enzymatic reactions including ATP production and DNA/RNA synthesis. Maintains genomic stability by supporting DNA repair enzymes and acts as a natural calcium channel blocker to prevent cellular stress and NF-kB overactivation. Required for proper activation of Vitamin D.

Key Evidence
  • Linear dose-response meta-analysis (N=1,168,756): each additional 100 mg/day associated with 6% reduced all-cause mortality and 5% reduced cancer mortality.
  • US NHANES diabetic cohort (N=6,004): high intake correlated with HR 0.62 for all-cause mortality.
  • Meta-analyses of RCTs: significant reductions in systolic and diastolic blood pressure (300–500 mg/day).
Preferred forms: Glycinate, Malate, or Threonate

Coenzyme Q10 (Ubiquinol)

100 – 300 mg / day
Mitochondrial Electron Transport • Lipid Antioxidant • Lysosomal Function

Fat-soluble electron shuttle in the inner mitochondrial membrane, transferring electrons from Complexes I and II to Complex III for ATP synthesis. Functions as a potent lipid-soluble antioxidant, protecting cell membranes and LDL from peroxidation, and supports vital lysosomal acidification for cellular waste disposal.

Key Evidence
  • Q-SYMBIO trial (N=420, heart failure, 106 weeks): 42.3% relative risk reduction for major adverse cardiovascular events (HR 0.50) and 44.4% reduction in all-cause mortality (HR 0.56).
  • KiSel-10 study (N=443, 5-year RCT): CoQ10 + Selenium resulted in 53% relative reduction in cardiovascular mortality (p=0.015). Validated at 12-year follow-up.
Ubiquinol form preferred for adults >40 due to superior absorption

Selenium

200 µg / day
Glutathione Peroxidase • Mitochondrial Fusion • Immune Modulation

Essential trace mineral acting as a cofactor for glutathione peroxidase and thioredoxin reductase. Neutralizes lipid hydroperoxides, promotes mitochondrial fusion to prevent energy imbalance, and modulates T-cell proliferation. Most effective when combined with CoQ10.

Key Evidence
  • KiSel-10 study (N=443 healthy elderly, 5-year RCT): Selenium + CoQ10 produced 53% relative reduction in cardiovascular mortality (p=0.015).
  • Dramatic cardiovascular survival benefit persisted and was validated in a 12-year follow-up study.
Organic selenium yeast form used in successful trials

Metformin

500 – 1,500 mg / day
AMPK Activation • mTOR Suppression • Autophagy • TET2 Stabilization

Partially inhibits Complex I of the mitochondrial electron transport chain, activating AMPK (the metabolic master switch), which suppresses mTOR, stimulates autophagy, and enhances insulin sensitivity. Modulates the epigenome by stabilizing TET2, preventing aberrant DNA methylation linked to genomic instability.

Key Evidence
  • Multi-institutional observational study (438 postmenopausal women): 30% lower risk of death before age 90 (exceptional longevity).
  • Systematic review of 12 RCTs (N=21,595) and 41 observational studies (N=1,029,389): 35% reduction in cancer mortality and 31% reduction in cancer incidence.
  • TAME (Targeting Aging with Metformin) trial is ongoing for definitive efficacy in healthy individuals.
Rx required • Monitor Vitamin B12 levels

Curcumin (Bioavailable Forms)

80 – 500 mg / day
NF-kB & COX-2 Inhibition • Nrf2 Activation • SIRT1 Upregulation • Mitophagy

Multitargeted anti-inflammatory polyphenol that inhibits NF-kB and COX-2, activates Nrf2 antioxidant defense, inhibits mTOR, and upregulates SIRT1 and autophagy. Induces mitophagy via the PINK1/Parkin pathway to clear damaged mitochondria.

Key Evidence
  • Umbrella meta-analysis (10 studies, N=5,870): significant decreases in CRP (ES -0.74), IL-6 (ES -1.07), TNF-α (ES -1.92).
  • Meta-analysis of 15 RCTs: curcumin significantly decreased IL-6 (p=0.02), hs-CRP (p=0.02), and MDA (p<0.001) (Tabrizi et al., 2018).
Tabrizi 2018 arrow_outward Requires high-bioavailability form (nanomicelle or phytosome)

Resveratrol (trans-Resveratrol)

500 – 1,000 mg / day
SIRT1 Allosteric Activation • Mitochondrial Biogenesis • Caloric Restriction Mimetic

Natural polyphenol that mimics caloric restriction by allosterically activating SIRT1, enhancing mitochondrial biogenesis, improving insulin sensitivity, and increasing cellular autophagy. Take with dietary fat; threshold >400 mg/day most effective.

Key Evidence
  • Umbrella review of 18 meta-analyses (up to N=5,878): significant reduction in body weight (-0.18 kg), BMI (-0.14 kg/m²), and waist circumference (-0.43 cm).
  • Meta-analyses confirm thresholds >400 mg/day to be most effective for metabolic outcomes.
trans-Resveratrol form • Take with dietary fat

High-Potential Longevity Compounds

Moderate to Emerging Evidence

These compounds demonstrate compelling mechanisms and promising early clinical data for extending human healthspan. They require additional large-scale replication before achieving full clinical validation. Active clinical trials are underway for most.

NMN (Nicotinamide Mononucleotide)

250 – 1,000 mg / day
NAD+ Precursor • Sirtuin Reactivation • Mitochondrial Rescue

Direct precursor to NAD+. Restores the cellular NAD+/NADH ratio, reactivating sirtuins (SIRT1 and SIRT3) to improve mitochondrial function, reduce oxidative stress, and stave off cellular senescence. The leading NAD+ precursor in longevity research.

Key Evidence
  • RCT (N=80, double-blind): All NMN groups (300/600/900 mg) significantly increased blood NAD+ at days 30 and 60 (all p≤0.001). Walking distance on 6-min test significantly higher vs. placebo (p<0.01). Biological age unchanged in NMN groups while it increased in placebo (p<0.05) (Yi et al., 2022).
  • Uthever™ NMN study: 38% increase in NAD+ levels after 60 days.
  • Meta-analysis of 10 RCTs in older adults: minimal functional impact on skeletal muscle strength or gait speed; pilot studies hint at improved insulin sensitivity in prediabetic women.

Evidence Level: Moderate. Strong for biomarker elevation (NAD+), but functional clinical endpoints still emerging. Long-term safety data in humans still accumulating.

Yi 2022 arrow_outward

Spermidine

1 – 3 mg / day
Caloric Restriction Mimetic • EP300 Inhibition • Autophagy Induction

Natural polyamine functioning as a caloric restriction mimetic. Induces cellular autophagy primarily by inhibiting EP300, an acetyltransferase that otherwise suppresses autophagy, initiating breakdown and recycling of damaged organelles and proteins. Spermidine levels decline with age, and higher dietary intake is independently associated with reduced mortality.

Key Evidence
  • Dietary epidemiological studies (N>800): higher spermidine intake independently associated with lower all-cause, cardiovascular, and cancer mortality.
  • Animal models: 10% increase in overall longevity.
  • High heritability (82%) of erythrocyte spermidine identified in twin metabolomics study; correlated with 59 metabolites and 22 proteins linked to longevity (Kaminsky et al., 2024, Aging Cell).

Evidence Level: Moderate. Epidemiological and animal data compelling; large-scale human intervention RCTs needed.

Kaminsky 2024 arrow_outward Madeo 2018 arrow_outward

Fisetin

20 mg/kg • Intermittent Protocol
Senolytic • PI3K/AKT/mTOR Inhibition • SASP Suppression

Potent senolytic flavonoid that selectively induces apoptosis in non-functioning senescent "zombie" cells by inhibiting pro-survival PI3K/AKT/mTOR pathways and suppressing the damaging Senescence-Associated Secretory Phenotype (SASP). Administered in intermittent "hit-and-run" protocols rather than daily.

Key Evidence
  • Progeroid and aged mice: intermittent treatment drastically reduced senescent cell burden, restored tissue homeostasis, and extended maximum lifespan.
  • Human pilot data: senescent cell clearance confirmed in human adipose explants.
  • AFFIRM-LITE clinical trial targeting frailty is ongoing.
  • Senolytic therapy in human brain organoids: reduced age-related inflammation and rejuvenated transcriptomic aging clocks (Aguado et al., 2023, Nature Aging).

Evidence Level: Emerging. Strong animal and pilot human data; awaiting results from AFFIRM-LITE and other ongoing trials.

Aguado 2023 arrow_outward

Quercetin

1,000 mg • Intermittent
Senolytic • NF-kB Inhibition • Immune Cell Clearance

Flavonoid senolytic that clears senescent immune cells and mitigates inflammation by inhibiting the NF-kB pathway, reducing pro-inflammatory cytokines. Most effective when combined with dasatinib in senolytic protocols or with other polyphenols (resveratrol, curcumin) for synergistic effects.

Key Evidence
  • Phase II clinical trial (N=32): quercetin + resveratrol + curcumin yielded 2.5% reduction in macular drusen volume (p=0.03) over 24 months in AMD patients.
  • Dasatinib + Quercetin combination: improved physical function in early human trials of senolytic therapy.

Evidence Level: Moderate. Animal and human RCT data available; larger longevity-specific trials needed.

Alpha-Ketoglutarate (Ca-AKG)

1,000 – 2,000 mg / day
Krebs Cycle Intermediate • TET Demethylase Cofactor • Epigenetic Fidelity

Intermediary in the Krebs cycle linking metabolism to gene regulation. Essential cofactor for TET DNA demethylases and JmjC histone demethylases, preserving epigenetic fidelity and promoting DNA repair while mimicking metabolic benefits of caloric restriction via FOXO1 and AMPK.

Key Evidence
  • Pilot study (N=42, average age 64, 7 months): Calcium-AKG demonstrated an average 8-year reduction in biological age measured by DNA methylation epigenetic clocks.
  • Mouse studies: AKG-fed females protected against age-dependent increases in circulating inflammatory cytokines; healthspan improvements with reduced frailty (Rhoads & Anderson, 2020, Cell Metabolism).

Evidence Level: Emerging. Compelling pilot study and animal data; larger human RCTs needed to confirm.

Rhoads 2020 arrow_outward Calcium-AKG form preferred for stability

Rapamycin (Low-Dose)

Dosage Under Investigation
mTORC1 Inhibition • Autophagy • NAD+/NADH Ratio • Mitochondrial Function

Direct pharmacological inhibitor of mTOR complex 1. Reduces cellular anabolic demand, strongly activates autophagy, improves mitochondrial function, and increases the NAD+/NADH ratio. The most robust life-extending intervention in preclinical geroscience across all model organisms tested.

Key Evidence
  • NIA Interventions Testing Program: rapamycin fed late in life profoundly extended lifespan of genetically heterogeneous mice.
  • Extends lifespan in yeast, worms, flies, and mice—the only compound to do so across all tested model organisms.
  • VIAging trial and other human safety/deceleration pilot trials currently underway.

Evidence Level: Emerging / Preclinical. Extremely strong animal data; human longevity trials are pending validation. Rx only with physician supervision.

Bioactive Peptides

Emerging Trend • High Research Activity

Peptides are short chains of amino acids (typically 2–50 residues) that act as highly specific signaling molecules in the body. Unlike traditional supplements, peptides bind to specific receptors on cell surfaces, triggering targeted intracellular cascades—activating growth factors, modulating immune responses, or enhancing mitochondrial function with remarkable precision.

How they work: Peptides mimic or augment endogenous signaling molecules. They are typically administered via subcutaneous injection, intranasal spray, or topical application because oral bioavailability is limited by gastric proteolysis. Their short half-lives and receptor specificity contribute to favorable safety profiles, though most remain in preclinical or early clinical investigation for longevity and regenerative applications.

BPC-157 (Body Protection Compound)

250 – 500 µg / day
Gastric Pentadecapeptide • NO System Modulation • Growth Factor Upregulation • Angiogenesis

A 15-amino-acid peptide (GEPPPGKPADDAGLV) derived from human gastric juice. BPC-157 is stable in gastric acid—unique among peptides—and operates through multiple mechanisms: upregulating growth factor receptors (VEGF, EGF), modulating the nitric oxide (NO) system, promoting angiogenesis, and counteracting organ damage. It accelerates healing of muscle, tendon, ligament, bone, and gastrointestinal tissue in preclinical models.

Key Evidence
  • Rat model: BPC-157 given locally or intraperitoneally accelerated post-injury muscle healing and restored full function at all investigated intervals (Novinscak et al., 2008).
  • Complete quadriceps transection model: systemic BPC-157 treatment induced prompt muscle healing with functional restoration (Staresinic et al., 2006).
  • Prolonged esophagitis model with sphincter failure: BPC-157 demonstrated significant anti-esophagitis therapeutic potential, remaining stable in gastric juice (Bratiević et al., 2006).
  • Phase II clinical trial for inflammatory bowel disease (IBD) has been conducted; further human trials ongoing.

Evidence Level: Moderate. Extensive preclinical data across dozens of animal models; limited but promising human trial data. Uniquely stable in gastric juice.

Thymosin Beta-4 (TB-500)

2 – 5 mg / 2× week
Actin Sequestration • Cell Migration • Cardiac Repair • Anti-Fibrotic

A naturally occurring 43-amino-acid peptide and the primary G-actin sequestering molecule in eukaryotic cells. Thymosin β4 promotes tissue repair by modulating actin dynamics to enable cell migration, reducing inflammation, and stimulating stem cell differentiation. It plays a critical role in wound healing, cardiac repair after ischemic injury, and muscle regeneration.

Key Evidence
  • Cardiac repair studies: Thymosin β4 activated resident cardiac progenitor cells and promoted myocardial repair following ischemic injury in animal models (Hara, 2011).
  • Demonstrated significant anti-fibrotic properties in multiple organ systems, reducing scar tissue formation.
  • Accelerates dermal wound healing by promoting keratinocyte migration and angiogenesis.
  • Phase II clinical trials completed for corneal wound healing (RegeneRx Biopharmaceuticals).

Evidence Level: Moderate. Strong animal data across cardiac, dermal, and corneal repair models. Phase II human trials in ophthalmology completed.

Hara 2011 arrow_outward

GHK-Cu (Copper Peptide)

Topical • 1 – 2% concentration
Collagen Synthesis • Tissue Remodeling • Anti-Inflammatory • Gene Expression Modulation

A naturally occurring tripeptide (Gly-His-Lys) found in human plasma, saliva, and urine that forms a high-affinity chelate with copper(II). Serum levels average 200 ng/mL at age 20 but decline to 80 ng/mL by age 60. GHK-Cu stimulates collagen I, III, and VI synthesis, promotes wound healing and tissue remodeling, and modulates the expression of over 4,000 human genes—resetting gene expression patterns toward a healthier state (Dou et al., 2020).

Key Evidence
  • In vitro and in vivo studies demonstrate prominent antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and tissue remodeling effects including promotion of wound healing and skin regeneration (Dou et al., 2020).
  • Preliminary observations suggest GHK can partially reverse cognitive impairment in aging mice by targeting anti-inflammatory and epigenetic pathways.
  • Human efficacy tests show benefits for improving skin repair, anti-aging, anti-wrinkle, and moisturizing properties (Liu et al., 2022).
  • Gene expression studies: resets expression of ~32% of human genes to a younger pattern.

Evidence Level: Moderate. Strong in vitro and animal data; human studies primarily in dermatology. Systemic anti-aging applications need larger clinical trials.

Dou 2020 arrow_outward Liu 2022 arrow_outward

Epitalon (AEDG Peptide)

5 – 10 mg / day • 10–20 day cycles
Telomerase Activation • Pineal Gland Regulation • Melatonin Synthesis • Antioxidant

A synthetic tetrapeptide (Ala-Glu-Asp-Gly) based on the amino acid composition of Epithalamin, a bovine pineal gland extract. First detected in physiological human pineal gland extract in 2017. Epitalon activates telomerase in human somatic cells, directly influences melatonin synthesis, modulates IL-2 mRNA levels, and has demonstrated geroprotective effects across 25 years of research (Araj et al., 2025).

Key Evidence
  • Telomerase-positive fibroblasts treated with Epitalon demonstrated high telomere lengths during the G1 phase, overcoming replicative limits (Araj et al., 2025).
  • 12-year randomized clinical study (elderly patients with coronary disease): 28% lower mortality, 2-fold lower cardiovascular mortality, and decreased functional age vs. control (Korkushko et al., 2006).
  • Epitalon effectively and selectively safeguarded aged human pineal cells from the aging process, confirming tissue-specific geroprotective activity.
  • Increases mitochondrial staining area by 1.5× in aging pineal gland cell cultures (Ivko et al., 2021).

Evidence Level: Moderate. Decades of in vitro and animal research; one long-term clinical study. Most evidence from Russian research groups. Replication by independent labs needed.

SS-31 (Elamipretide)

Dosage Under Investigation
Cardiolipin Stabilization • Mitochondrial Inner Membrane • Electron Transport Chain • ROS Reduction

A Szeto-Schiller tetrapeptide (D-Arg-Dmt-Lys-Phe-NH2) that selectively concentrates >1000-fold in the inner mitochondrial membrane. SS-31 stabilizes cardiolipin—a phospholipid critical for cristae structure and electron transport chain supercomplex assembly—thereby improving ATP synthesis, reducing reactive oxygen species, and rescuing mitochondrial dysfunction. Among the most promising therapeutic compounds for mitochondrial diseases.

Key Evidence
  • Human failing heart tissue: Elamipretide improved mitochondrial function in the failing human heart, restoring respiratory capacity (Chatfield et al., 2019).
  • Phase II/III clinical trials for Barth syndrome, primary mitochondrial myopathy, and heart failure with reduced ejection fraction.
  • Animal studies: reverses age-related mitochondrial dysfunction, improves cardiac function, and reduces oxidative damage across multiple organ systems.
  • FDA granted Rare Pediatric Disease designation and Fast Track designation for Barth syndrome.

Evidence Level: Strong (for mitochondrial diseases). Multiple Phase II/III clinical trials. FDA designations granted. Longevity applications in early investigation.

Chatfield 2019 arrow_outward Rx • Clinical trials ongoing

KPV (Alpha-MSH Tripeptide)

200 – 500 µg / day
NF-kB Inhibition • PepT1 Transport • Anti-Inflammatory • Gut Barrier Integrity

The C-terminal tripeptide (Lys-Pro-Val) of alpha-melanocyte stimulating hormone (α-MSH), retaining potent anti-inflammatory activity without melanocortin receptor dependency. KPV is actively transported into colonocytes via the PepT1 transporter, where it enters the nucleus and inhibits NF-κB activation—the master inflammatory switch. This direct intracellular mechanism makes it uniquely effective for intestinal inflammation.

Key Evidence
  • PepT1-mediated uptake study: KPV transport into colonocytes reduces intestinal inflammation via direct NF-kB inhibition in the nucleus (Dalmasso et al., 2008).
  • Two murine colitis models: KPV showed significant anti-inflammatory effects partially independent of MC1R signaling, suggesting multiple therapeutic mechanisms (Laroui et al., 2008).
  • α-MSH and its fragments demonstrate broad-spectrum antimicrobial activity in addition to anti-inflammatory effects.

Evidence Level: Emerging. Strong mechanistic and animal data for gut inflammation. Human clinical trials needed for IBD and systemic inflammation applications.

Dalmasso 2008 arrow_outward Laroui 2008 arrow_outward

Selank (TP-7)

250 – 500 µg • Intranasal
GABAA Allosteric Modulation • Anxiolytic • Immunomodulatory • Nootropic

A synthetic heptapeptide combining a short fragment (Thr-Lys-Pro-Arg) of human immunoglobulin G heavy chain with the stabilizing tripeptide Pro-Gly-Pro. Selank possesses pronounced anxiolytic activity comparable to classical benzodiazepines but without addiction, withdrawal, or sedation. It modulates GABAA receptors allosterically, enhancing endogenous GABA binding, while simultaneously exerting immunomodulatory and nootropic effects.

Key Evidence
  • Chronic mild stress model: combined Selank + diazepam was most effective in reducing anxiety; Selank alone was most effective in reducing elevated anxiety from chronic drug administration (Kasian et al., 2017).
  • Selank influences specific binding of GABA to GABAA receptors, modulating affinity of endogenous ligands (Filatova et al., 2017).
  • Approved in Russia as an anxiolytic medication; clinical trials demonstrated efficacy in generalized anxiety disorder and neurasthenia.
  • No addiction, sedation, or withdrawal syndrome reported—superior safety profile to benzodiazepines.

Evidence Level: Moderate. Approved medication in Russia. Clinical trial data available. Wider international regulatory approval and replication needed.

Kasian 2017 arrow_outward Filatova 2017 arrow_outward

FOXO4-DRI (Senolytic Peptide)

Dosage Under Investigation
FOXO4-p53 Disruption • Senescent Cell Apoptosis • D-Retro-Inverso Peptide • Senolytic

A D-retro-inverso peptide engineered to disrupt the interaction between FOXO4 and p53 in senescent cells. In senescent "zombie" cells, FOXO4 sequesters p53 away from mitochondria, preventing apoptosis. FOXO4-DRI competes with endogenous FOXO4, liberating p53 to trigger selective apoptosis exclusively in senescent cells while leaving healthy cells unaffected. The D-retro-inverso modification renders the peptide protease-resistant with enhanced bioavailability.

Key Evidence
  • Precise disruption of FOXO4-p53 complex selectively induces apoptosis in senescent cells while sparing non-senescent cells (EBioMedicine, 2021).
  • In vitro expanded human chondrocytes: FOXO4-DRI selectively removes senescent cells, improving quality of cartilage tissue for autologous chondrocyte implantation (Faust et al., 2021).
  • Aged mice: treatment restored fitness, fur density, and renal function by clearance of senescent cells.
  • Molecular modelling studies are optimizing next-generation senolytic peptides based on the FOXO4-p53 interface.

Evidence Level: Emerging. Strong mechanistic basis and in vitro/animal data. No human clinical trials yet. High cost and peptide delivery remain challenges.

EBioMedicine 2021 arrow_outward Faust 2021 arrow_outward

CJC-1295 / Ipamorelin

100 – 300 µg each / day
GHRH Analog • Ghrelin Mimetic • Pulsatile GH Release • IGF-1 Elevation

A synergistic peptide combination. CJC-1295 is a modified growth hormone-releasing hormone (GHRH) analog with Drug Affinity Complex (DAC) technology that extends its half-life to 6–8 days. Ipamorelin is a selective growth hormone secretagogue (ghrelin mimetic) that triggers pulsatile GH release without significantly affecting cortisol or prolactin. Together, they amplify the natural GH pulse, promoting lean muscle, fat metabolism, deeper sleep, and tissue recovery.

Key Evidence
  • CJC-1295 DAC demonstrated sustained elevation of GH and IGF-1 levels for 6–8 days after a single injection in healthy adults.
  • Ipamorelin is the most selective GH secretagogue known, with minimal effect on ACTH, cortisol, or prolactin compared to GHRP-6 and GHRP-2.
  • Combination protocols widely used in anti-aging medicine for body composition optimization, sleep quality, and recovery.
  • Growth hormone secretagogues are classified as prohibited substances by WADA; glycine-modified variants identified in seized preparations (Gajda et al., 2018).

Evidence Level: Moderate. Human pharmacokinetic data available. Long-term safety and longevity-specific RCTs lacking. WADA-prohibited in sport.

Gajda 2018 arrow_outward Rx required • WADA-prohibited

Dihexa (PNB-0408)

Dosage Under Investigation
HGF/c-Met Activation • Synaptogenesis • Spinogenesis • Blood-Brain Barrier Permeant

An orally active, blood-brain barrier permeant, metabolically stabilized angiotensin IV analog (N-hexanoic-Tyr-Ile-(6)-aminohexanoic amide). Dihexa activates the hepatocyte growth factor (HGF)/c-Met receptor system—a powerful mediator of synaptogenesis, neurogenesis, and neuroprotection. It stimulates hippocampal spinogenesis and the formation of new functional synaptic connections, with potential applications in Alzheimer’s disease and cognitive decline (McCoy et al., 2013).

Key Evidence
  • Scopolamine and aged rat models: Dihexa reversed cognitive deficits and augmented hippocampal synaptogenesis when administered orally (McCoy et al., 2013).
  • Demonstrated ability to induce long-term potentiation and augment memory consolidation and retrieval in animal models of Alzheimer’s disease (Wright & Harding, 2020).
  • Orally active with confirmed blood-brain barrier permeability—a major advantage over protein neurotrophins like NGF and BDNF which cannot cross the BBB.
  • Described as up to 10 million times more potent than BDNF in promoting synaptogenesis.

Evidence Level: Emerging. Preclinical animal data only. No human clinical trials. Long-term safety profile unknown. Extremely potent—requires caution.

How Peptides Work

Unlike small-molecule supplements, peptides act as precision signaling molecules that bind specific cell-surface receptors, triggering targeted intracellular cascades.

1. Receptor Binding
Peptide docks with membrane receptor (GPCR, tyrosine kinase, or ion channel).
2. Signal Transduction
Intracellular cascade activates (MAPK, PI3K/AKT, JAK/STAT, NF-kB modulation).
3. Gene Expression
Transcription factors alter protein synthesis—growth factors, cytokines, enzymes.
4. Biological Effect
Tissue repair, immune modulation, neurogenesis, or senescent cell clearance.

Peptide Safety Notice

Most therapeutic peptides are investigational compounds not yet approved by the FDA or EMA for longevity indications. Quality, purity, and sterility of research-grade peptides vary widely between suppliers. Several peptides (CJC-1295, Ipamorelin) are prohibited by WADA. Always source from reputable compounding pharmacies and consult a physician before use. This is not medical advice.
