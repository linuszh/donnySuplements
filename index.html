Proven Longevity Compounds

Strong RCT & Meta-Analysis Evidence

Vitamin D3 (Cholecalciferol)

800 – 2,000 IU / day
Genomic Stability • Immune Regulation • Anti-Inflammaging

Acts as a secosteroid hormone influencing ~3% of the human genome via the Vitamin D Receptor (VDR). Reduces oxidative stress and chronic inflammaging, preserving telomere length and genomic stability. Essential for bone homeostasis, innate immune function, and endocrine regulation.

Key Evidence
  • Meta-analysis of RCTs (N=74,655): statistically significant reduction in cancer mortality (RR 0.85); all-cause mortality RR 0.98.
  • VITAL trial (N=25,871): emerging signal for reduced cancer mortality (HR 0.83).
  • Systematic review (Zhang et al., 2019, BMJ): Vitamin D3 supplementation associated with decreased total mortality across 52 RCTs.
  • Meta-analysis of 10 RCTs: significantly reduced total cancer mortality (RR 0.87, 95% CI 0.79–0.96, p = 0.005) (Keum et al., 2019).
Zhang 2019 arrow_outward Keum 2019 arrow_outward DOI: 10.1136/bmj.l4673

Omega-3 Fatty Acids (EPA + DHA)

1 – 4 g EPA+DHA / day
Pro-Resolving Mediators • NF-kB Inhibition • Telomere Preservation

EPA and DHA serve as precursors for specialized pro-resolving mediators (SPMs) like resolvins and protectins, which actively terminate inflammatory responses. They modulate cell signaling by inhibiting the pro-inflammatory NF-kB pathway and preserve telomere length by mitigating oxidative DNA damage.

Key Evidence
  • Pooled analysis of 17 prospective studies (N=42,466, Nature Communications): 18% reduction in all-cause mortality associated with higher blood omega-3 levels over 16-year median follow-up (Harris et al., 2021).
  • DO-HEALTH study (N=777): Omega-3s reduced biological age across multiple epigenetic clocks (PhenoAge, GrimAge2) and slowed the pace of aging.
  • Meta-analysis of 38 RCTs: confirmed significant reduction in cardiovascular mortality.
  • Network meta-analysis of 55 RCTs: significant CVD death reduction (RR 0.92, 95% CI 0.87–0.98) (Kim et al., 2020).
Harris 2021 arrow_outward Kim 2020 arrow_outward DOI: 10.1038/s41467-021-22370-2

Magnesium

250 – 450 mg / day
300+ Enzymatic Reactions • DNA Repair • Calcium Channel Blockade

Essential catalytic cofactor for over 300 enzymatic reactions including ATP production and DNA/RNA synthesis. Maintains genomic stability by supporting DNA repair enzymes and acts as a natural calcium channel blocker to prevent cellular stress and NF-kB overactivation. Required for proper activation of Vitamin D.

Key Evidence
  • Linear dose-response meta-analysis (N=1,168,756): each additional 100 mg/day associated with 6% reduced all-cause mortality and 5% reduced cancer mortality.
  • US NHANES diabetic cohort (N=6,004): high intake correlated with HR 0.62 for all-cause mortality.
  • Meta-analyses of RCTs: significant reductions in systolic and diastolic blood pressure (300–500 mg/day).
Preferred forms: Glycinate, Malate, or Threonate

Coenzyme Q10 (Ubiquinol)

100 – 300 mg / day
Mitochondrial Electron Transport • Lipid Antioxidant • Lysosomal Function

Fat-soluble electron shuttle in the inner mitochondrial membrane, transferring electrons from Complexes I and II to Complex III for ATP synthesis. Functions as a potent lipid-soluble antioxidant, protecting cell membranes and LDL from peroxidation, and supports vital lysosomal acidification for cellular waste disposal.

Key Evidence
  • Q-SYMBIO trial (N=420, heart failure, 106 weeks): 42.3% relative risk reduction for major adverse cardiovascular events (HR 0.50) and 44.4% reduction in all-cause mortality (HR 0.56).
  • KiSel-10 study (N=443, 5-year RCT): CoQ10 + Selenium resulted in 53% relative reduction in cardiovascular mortality (p=0.015). Validated at 12-year follow-up.
Ubiquinol form preferred for adults >40 due to superior absorption

Selenium

200 µg / day
Glutathione Peroxidase • Mitochondrial Fusion • Immune Modulation

Essential trace mineral acting as a cofactor for glutathione peroxidase and thioredoxin reductase. Neutralizes lipid hydroperoxides, promotes mitochondrial fusion to prevent energy imbalance, and modulates T-cell proliferation. Most effective when combined with CoQ10.

Key Evidence
  • KiSel-10 study (N=443 healthy elderly, 5-year RCT): Selenium + CoQ10 produced 53% relative reduction in cardiovascular mortality (p=0.015).
  • Dramatic cardiovascular survival benefit persisted and was validated in a 12-year follow-up study.
Organic selenium yeast form used in successful trials

Metformin

500 – 1,500 mg / day
AMPK Activation • mTOR Suppression • Autophagy • TET2 Stabilization

Partially inhibits Complex I of the mitochondrial electron transport chain, activating AMPK (the metabolic master switch), which suppresses mTOR, stimulates autophagy, and enhances insulin sensitivity. Modulates the epigenome by stabilizing TET2, preventing aberrant DNA methylation linked to genomic instability.

Key Evidence
  • Multi-institutional observational study (438 postmenopausal women): 30% lower risk of death before age 90 (exceptional longevity).
  • Systematic review of 12 RCTs (N=21,595) and 41 observational studies (N=1,029,389): 35% reduction in cancer mortality and 31% reduction in cancer incidence.
  • TAME (Targeting Aging with Metformin) trial is ongoing for definitive efficacy in healthy individuals.
Rx required • Monitor Vitamin B12 levels

Curcumin (Bioavailable Forms)

80 – 500 mg / day
NF-kB & COX-2 Inhibition • Nrf2 Activation • SIRT1 Upregulation • Mitophagy

Multitargeted anti-inflammatory polyphenol that inhibits NF-kB and COX-2, activates Nrf2 antioxidant defense, inhibits mTOR, and upregulates SIRT1 and autophagy. Induces mitophagy via the PINK1/Parkin pathway to clear damaged mitochondria.

Key Evidence
  • Umbrella meta-analysis (10 studies, N=5,870): significant decreases in CRP (ES -0.74), IL-6 (ES -1.07), TNF-α (ES -1.92).
  • Meta-analysis of 15 RCTs: curcumin significantly decreased IL-6 (p=0.02), hs-CRP (p=0.02), and MDA (p<0.001) (Tabrizi et al., 2018).
Tabrizi 2018 arrow_outward Requires high-bioavailability form (nanomicelle or phytosome)

Resveratrol (trans-Resveratrol)

500 – 1,000 mg / day
SIRT1 Allosteric Activation • Mitochondrial Biogenesis • Caloric Restriction Mimetic

Natural polyphenol that mimics caloric restriction by allosterically activating SIRT1, enhancing mitochondrial biogenesis, improving insulin sensitivity, and increasing cellular autophagy. Take with dietary fat; threshold >400 mg/day most effective.

Key Evidence
  • Umbrella review of 18 meta-analyses (up to N=5,878): significant reduction in body weight (-0.18 kg), BMI (-0.14 kg/m²), and waist circumference (-0.43 cm).
  • Meta-analyses confirm thresholds >400 mg/day to be most effective for metabolic outcomes.
trans-Resveratrol form • Take with dietary fat

High-Potential Longevity Compounds

Moderate to Emerging Evidence

These compounds demonstrate compelling mechanisms and promising early clinical data for extending human healthspan. They require additional large-scale replication before achieving full clinical validation. Active clinical trials are underway for most.

NMN (Nicotinamide Mononucleotide)

250 – 1,000 mg / day
NAD+ Precursor • Sirtuin Reactivation • Mitochondrial Rescue

Direct precursor to NAD+. Restores the cellular NAD+/NADH ratio, reactivating sirtuins (SIRT1 and SIRT3) to improve mitochondrial function, reduce oxidative stress, and stave off cellular senescence. The leading NAD+ precursor in longevity research.

Key Evidence
  • RCT (N=80, double-blind): All NMN groups (300/600/900 mg) significantly increased blood NAD+ at days 30 and 60 (all p≤0.001). Walking distance on 6-min test significantly higher vs. placebo (p<0.01). Biological age unchanged in NMN groups while it increased in placebo (p<0.05) (Yi et al., 2022).
  • Uthever™ NMN study: 38% increase in NAD+ levels after 60 days.
  • Meta-analysis of 10 RCTs in older adults: minimal functional impact on skeletal muscle strength or gait speed; pilot studies hint at improved insulin sensitivity in prediabetic women.

Evidence Level: Moderate. Strong for biomarker elevation (NAD+), but functional clinical endpoints still emerging. Long-term safety data in humans still accumulating.

Yi 2022 arrow_outward

Spermidine

1 – 3 mg / day
Caloric Restriction Mimetic • EP300 Inhibition • Autophagy Induction

Natural polyamine functioning as a caloric restriction mimetic. Induces cellular autophagy primarily by inhibiting EP300, an acetyltransferase that otherwise suppresses autophagy, initiating breakdown and recycling of damaged organelles and proteins. Spermidine levels decline with age, and higher dietary intake is independently associated with reduced mortality.

Key Evidence
  • Dietary epidemiological studies (N>800): higher spermidine intake independently associated with lower all-cause, cardiovascular, and cancer mortality.
  • Animal models: 10% increase in overall longevity.
  • High heritability (82%) of erythrocyte spermidine identified in twin metabolomics study; correlated with 59 metabolites and 22 proteins linked to longevity (Kaminsky et al., 2024, Aging Cell).

Evidence Level: Moderate. Epidemiological and animal data compelling; large-scale human intervention RCTs needed.

Fisetin

20 mg/kg • Intermittent Protocol
Senolytic • PI3K/AKT/mTOR Inhibition • SASP Suppression

Potent senolytic flavonoid that selectively induces apoptosis in non-functioning senescent "zombie" cells by inhibiting pro-survival PI3K/AKT/mTOR pathways and suppressing the damaging Senescence-Associated Secretory Phenotype (SASP). Administered in intermittent "hit-and-run" protocols rather than daily.

Key Evidence
  • Progeroid and aged mice: intermittent treatment drastically reduced senescent cell burden, restored tissue homeostasis, and extended maximum lifespan.
  • Human pilot data: senescent cell clearance confirmed in human adipose explants.
  • AFFIRM-LITE clinical trial targeting frailty is ongoing.
  • Senolytic therapy in human brain organoids: reduced age-related inflammation and rejuvenated transcriptomic aging clocks (Aguado et al., 2023, Nature Aging).

Evidence Level: Emerging. Strong animal and pilot human data; awaiting results from AFFIRM-LITE and other ongoing trials.

Aguado 2023 arrow_outward

Quercetin

1,000 mg • Intermittent
Senolytic • NF-kB Inhibition • Immune Cell Clearance

Flavonoid senolytic that clears senescent immune cells and mitigates inflammation by inhibiting the NF-kB pathway, reducing pro-inflammatory cytokines. Most effective when combined with dasatinib in senolytic protocols or with other polyphenols (resveratrol, curcumin) for synergistic effects.

Key Evidence
  • Phase II clinical trial (N=32): quercetin + resveratrol + curcumin yielded 2.5% reduction in macular drusen volume (p=0.03) over 24 months in AMD patients.
  • Dasatinib + Quercetin combination: improved physical function in early human trials of senolytic therapy.

Evidence Level: Moderate. Animal and human RCT data available; larger longevity-specific trials needed.

Alpha-Ketoglutarate (Ca-AKG)

1,000 – 2,000 mg / day
Krebs Cycle Intermediate • TET Demethylase Cofactor • Epigenetic Fidelity

Intermediary in the Krebs cycle linking metabolism to gene regulation. Essential cofactor for TET DNA demethylases and JmjC histone demethylases, preserving epigenetic fidelity and promoting DNA repair while mimicking metabolic benefits of caloric restriction via FOXO1 and AMPK.

Key Evidence
  • Pilot study (N=42, average age 64, 7 months): Calcium-AKG demonstrated an average 8-year reduction in biological age measured by DNA methylation epigenetic clocks.
  • Mouse studies: AKG-fed females protected against age-dependent increases in circulating inflammatory cytokines; healthspan improvements with reduced frailty (Rhoads & Anderson, 2020, Cell Metabolism).

Evidence Level: Emerging. Compelling pilot study and animal data; larger human RCTs needed to confirm.

Rhoads 2020 arrow_outward Calcium-AKG form preferred for stability

Rapamycin (Low-Dose)

Dosage Under Investigation
mTORC1 Inhibition • Autophagy • NAD+/NADH Ratio • Mitochondrial Function

Direct pharmacological inhibitor of mTOR complex 1. Reduces cellular anabolic demand, strongly activates autophagy, improves mitochondrial function, and increases the NAD+/NADH ratio. The most robust life-extending intervention in preclinical geroscience across all model organisms tested.

Key Evidence
  • NIA Interventions Testing Program: rapamycin fed late in life profoundly extended lifespan of genetically heterogeneous mice.
  • Extends lifespan in yeast, worms, flies, and mice—the only compound to do so across all tested model organisms.
  • VIAging trial and other human safety/deceleration pilot trials currently underway.

Evidence Level: Emerging / Preclinical. Extremely strong animal data; human longevity trials are pending validation. Rx only with physician supervision.
